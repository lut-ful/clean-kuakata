# 500 Volunteers. One Beach. One Morning.

On June 5, 2026, we need **500 people** to show up at Kuakata Beach at 7:00 AM.

That's it. No experience. No special equipment. Just you, a pair of biodegradable gloves, and a few hours on one of the most beautiful beaches in Bangladesh.

## What Your Day Looks Like

**7:00 AM** — You arrive at Zero Point, Kuakata Beach. The sun is rising. You pick up your zone assignment, your T-shirt, and your breakfast. You meet your team leader.

**8:00 AM** — Clean-up begins. You and your team of 15 work your assigned beach zone. Some collect plastic by hand. Some sort by material type. Some record the data. All of it matters.

**10:00 AM** — Midpoint checkpoint. The numbers are already impressive. Zone coordinators update the live count.

**12:00 PM** — Wrap-up. The waste bags are full. The beach looks different. You helped do that.

**12:30 PM** — Closing ceremony. The total weight is announced. The crowd reacts. Someone takes a photo of you holding a full bag and grinning. That photo ends up somewhere meaningful.

## What We Provide

You don't need to bring anything except yourself and your energy.

- ✓ Volunteer T-shirt (yours to keep)
- ✓ Biodegradable collection bags and gloves
- ✓ Breakfast and drinking water
- ✓ Zone assignment and team briefing
- ✓ Certificate of participation
- ✓ Full event-day media coverage

## Four Ways to Volunteer

**Clean-Up Crew** — The front line. Collect, sort, bag.

**Data Recorder** — Weigh and log by material type. Your numbers become our impact report.

**Media Volunteer** — Photograph, film, live post. Show the country what Bangladesh did.

**Zone Coordinator** — Lead a team of 10–15. For students with leadership experience.
