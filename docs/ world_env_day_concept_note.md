**CONCEPT NOTE**

**Plastic-Free Kuakata**

***World Environment Day Beach Clean-Up 2026***

*Theme: \'Save Kuakata, Save the Ocean\'*

**Event Date:** 5 June 2026\
**Location:** Kuakata Beach, Patuakhali, Bangladesh\
**Organized by:** **Active Mind** & **QualFix**

**1. Background**

Kuakata---often called the *"Daughter of the Sea"*---is one of
Bangladesh's most iconic coastal destinations and one of the very few
places in the world where visitors can view both sunrise and sunset over
the open ocean. However, rapid tourism growth, inadequate waste systems,
and widespread use of single-use plastics have increasingly threatened
Kuakata's fragile coastal ecosystem.

Large quantities of plastic bottles, food packaging, polythene bags,
discarded nylon fishing nets, and general waste now accumulate along the
shoreline. This pollution poses serious risks to:

-   Marine biodiversity

-   Fisheries and local livelihoods

-   Coastal ecosystems

-   Tourism, the local economy, and public health

World Environment Day offers a meaningful opportunity to unite
communities, institutions, and corporate partners to take action. In
response, **Active Mind** (youth engagement organization) and
**QualFix** (technology & social impact partner) propose the
**Plastic-Free Kuakata -- Environment Day Beach Clean-Up 2026**, a
large-scale, community-driven initiative to restore the coastline and
promote responsible environmental behavior.

**2. Objectives**

The initiative aims to achieve the following outcomes:

**Environmental Objectives**

-   Remove plastic and non-biodegradable waste from Kuakata Beach.

-   Reduce ocean-bound plastics through community-led action.

-   Promote sustainable waste management practices in coastal tourism
    > zones.

**Community & Educational Objectives**

-   Raise awareness about the impacts of plastic pollution on marine
    > life.

-   Engage local youth, schools, colleges, and volunteers in
    > environmental action.

-   Strengthen community stewardship of natural resources.

**Corporate Engagement Objectives**

-   Mobilize CSR participation from environmentally responsible
    > organizations.

-   Foster public--private partnerships to support long-term coastal
    > conservation.

**4. Key Activities**

**Waste Collection**

Volunteers will collect and sort the following items:

-   Plastic bottles

-   Plastic bags and wrappers

-   Nylon fishing nets ("ghost nets")

-   General garbage and debris

**Waste Sorting & Management**

All collected waste will be:

1.  **Sorted** by material type

2.  **Packed** using biodegradable bags

3.  **Transported** to local recycling or disposal partners

4.  **Processed/Recycled** through regional recycling vendors

This aligns with the project's emphasis on sustainability and circular
waste management.
